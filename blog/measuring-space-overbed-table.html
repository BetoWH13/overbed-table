Measuring Space for an Overbed Table: Tips on Selecting the Right Size and Dimensions

Learn how to properly measure your space and select an overbed table with the perfect dimensions for your environment. This comprehensive guide covers everything from basic measurements to advanced considerations for optimal fit and functionality.

Choosing the right overbed table involves more than just picking a design you like—it's crucial to ensure the table fits your environment without creating unnecessary clutter. Measuring your space and understanding the dimensions of overbed tables can help you select one that is both functional and well-suited to your room layout.

This guide provides tips for measuring your space and selecting an overbed table with the ideal size and dimensions for your needs.

Why Measuring Your Space Matters

1. Prevents Crowding

Ensures the table fits comfortably without obstructing other furniture or walkways.

2. Maximizes Functionality

A properly sized table provides ample surface area for your activities without overwhelming the room.

3. Enhances Safety

Ensures there's enough space for smooth mobility around the table, reducing the risk of accidents.

Step-by-Step Guide to Measuring Space for an Overbed Table

1. Measure the Bed or Chair Width

Why: Overbed tables are designed to slide over the bed or chair, so their base width must be compatible.

How:

  • Measure the total width of your bed or chair, including any rails, armrests, or attachments
  • Add 2–3 inches on each side for clearance

2. Measure Bed or Chair Height

Why: The table must be tall enough to clear the bed or chair but adjustable to a comfortable working height.

How:

  • Measure the height from the floor to the top of the mattress or seat cushion
  • For beds with adjustable frames, measure the height at its typical setting

3. Measure Available Floor Space

Why: The table's base must fit under the bed or around the chair without obstruction.

How:

  • Check for obstacles like bed frames, recliner bases, or under-bed storage
  • Measure the depth and width of the space where the table's base will sit

4. Account for Room Layout

Why: Ensure the table doesn't block pathways or create a cramped environment.

How:

  • Measure the total room dimensions and identify where the table will primarily be used
  • Check clearance around other furniture, such as nightstands or desks

5. Consider Mobility Needs

Why: Mobile overbed tables require enough space to move freely between rooms.

How:

  • Measure door widths and hallway clearances to ensure the table can pass through comfortably

Key Dimensions to Consider

1. Tabletop Size

  • Small Tables: Ideal for compact spaces; suitable for basic tasks like eating or reading
  • Medium Tables: Provide more surface area for activities like writing, using a laptop, or organizing supplies
  • Large Tables: Suitable for users who need space for multiple items, such as medical equipment or crafting tools

2. Base Width

Ensure the base width matches or exceeds the bed or chair width for proper positioning.

3. Height Range

Look for tables with a height range that accommodates your bed or chair, especially if they have adjustable features.

4. Base Depth

A deeper base may be required for stability but should fit comfortably under the furniture.

Tips for Selecting the Right Overbed Table

1. Match the Table to Your Activities

  • For simple tasks like eating or reading, a smaller table may suffice
  • For multitasking, opt for a medium or large table with ample surface area

2. Prioritize Adjustability

Choose a table with adjustable height and tilt to ensure ergonomic comfort.

3. Opt for Space-Saving Designs

For tight spaces, look for tables with compact or foldable features.

4. Check Mobility Features

If you need to move the table frequently, ensure it has smooth-rolling casters and a slim profile.

5. Consider Room Style

Select a table with a design and finish that complements your room's aesthetic.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Not Measuring Clearance

Forgetting to measure under the bed or chair can result in a table that doesn't fit properly.

2. Choosing the Wrong Height

A table that's too short or too tall will be uncomfortable to use.

3. Ignoring Pathways

Overlooking the room layout can lead to a cluttered and inconvenient setup.

4. Overlooking Weight Capacity

Ensure the table can handle the weight of your intended items, such as laptops or medical equipment.

Practical Example

Imagine you have a queen-size bed with a mattress height of 22 inches and under-bed storage bins that leave only 3 inches of clearance. You'd need:

  • A table with an adjustable height range starting above 22 inches
  • A base depth that can clear the storage bins
  • A tabletop wide enough to span the bed (approximately 60 inches) without overwhelming the room

Conclusion

Selecting the right overbed table begins with accurate measurements of your space and a clear understanding of your needs. By considering factors like bed or chair dimensions, room layout, and mobility requirements, you can find a table that enhances functionality without compromising comfort or safety. Overbed tables designed for specific spaces ensure you make the most of your environment, whether for healthcare, work, or daily activities.

With the proper measurements and a thoughtful selection process, an overbed table can seamlessly integrate into your space, offering convenience and practicality for years to come.
